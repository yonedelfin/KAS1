Models of Migration

According to anthropologist Henry Otley Beyer, the early prehistoric Philippines was populated by migrants including the Dawn Man, Aetas, Indonesians, and Malaysians.

At least 40,000 years ago, Negritos lived on a continental shelf called Sundaland. The lineage of this indigenous group can be traced to the First Sundaland People (Jinam et al., 2017).

These nations were collectively known as Austronesia. At around 1500 to 1000 BCE, they reached the Batanes Islands through boats and sails. This event is known as the Austronesian Expansion (Bellwood et al., 2006).
