Precolonial Politics

Trade and control of commerce with neighboring populations were important for the pre-colonial economy. Chiefdoms were the main political unit per area so there was no organized form of government which resulted in political division. Prior to the Spanish era, there was no concept of a single Philippines, we only had different chiefdoms without central unity.

Abinales, P. N., & Amoroso, D. J. (2017). State and Society in the Philippines. Rowman & Littlefield.
